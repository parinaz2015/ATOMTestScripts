Help Units

Be +

Vacuum wavelength for all transitions are provided in nm. Transition rates are in units of s\(^{-1}\) and lifetime in s. The absolute values of the reduced electric-dipole (E1) matrix elements are in atomic units (\(ea_0\)), where \(e\) is elementary charge and \(a_0\) is the Bohr radius. Electric quadrupole matrix elements and electric quadrupole moment values are given in atomic units, \(ea_0^2\), where \(e\) is elementary charge and \(a_0\) is the Bohr radius. Magnetic dipole matrix elements are given in Bohr magnetons, \(\mu_B\). We use the conventional system of atomic units, a.u., in which \(e\), the electron mass \(m_e\), and the reduced Planck constant have the numerical value 1, and the electric constant \(\epsilon_0\) has the numerical value \(1/(4\pi)\).
